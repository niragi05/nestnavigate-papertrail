# 📄 Paper Trail Sprint

## ✨ Feature Name & Description

**Feature Name:** Paper Trail Sprint

**Description:**  
Paper Trail Sprint is a simple, guided game feature that helps homebuyers understand which documents they need at each stage of the homebuying journey.

The idea comes from a very real frustration. When renewing a passport, it took digging through Reddit posts, YouTube videos, and repeated cross checking just to figure out which documents were needed. Even after all that effort, one required document was still missed. That was stressful for a process with only one main step.

Buying a home is much more complex. It has many stages, more paperwork, and higher stakes. Missing the right document at the wrong time can slow things down, create confusion, and make an already emotional process feel even harder.

Paper Trail Sprint turns that confusion into a clear, easy experience. Instead of overwhelming users with long lists, it breaks the process into small, manageable stages. Players move through milestones like getting pre approved, making an offer, underwriting, and closing, while learning which documents matter at each point. The goal is to make paperwork feel less intimidating and much easier to understand. 🏡🗂️

Above all, this feature is designed around **ease of use**. It should feel helpful, calm, and clear from the very first screen.

---

## 🎯 Engagement Hypothesis

If homebuyers are given a lightweight, interactive way to learn what documents are needed and when, they will feel more confident and less overwhelmed during the homebuying process.

Paper Trail Sprint is engaging because it turns a stressful topic into something approachable. Instead of reading static content, users actively sort, match, or collect the right documents for each stage. This makes the learning process feel more hands on and memorable.

The feature is also likely to keep users engaged because it gives them a sense of progress. Each completed stage feels like a small win. That feeling of moving forward can reduce anxiety and encourage users to continue learning.

In simple terms, the feature works because it answers a very practical question:

**"What do I need right now, and what comes next?"** 📌

When users feel that the product is helping them avoid mistakes and stay organized, they are more likely to return and trust the platform.

---

## 📚 Educational Value

Paper Trail Sprint teaches users the document side of homebuying in a way that is easier to absorb than traditional checklists or articles.

It helps users learn:

**Which documents are needed**  
Such as pay stubs, tax returns, bank statements, proof of identity, proof of employment, and other common paperwork.

**When those documents are needed**  
Not every document matters at every stage. The feature teaches timing, which is often where confusion happens.

**Why each document matters**  
A short explanation can help users understand the purpose behind each item, which makes the information easier to remember.

**How to prepare ahead of time**  
By seeing what is coming next, users can feel more in control and less rushed.

This feature does not try to replace professional advice. Instead, it helps users build confidence, reduce surprises, and feel more prepared for conversations with lenders, agents, and other people involved in the process. 💡

---

## 🔗 Platform Integration

Paper Trail Sprint is designed to feel like a natural part of the Nest Navigate experience, not a separate mini app.

**Learning journey integration**  
The game can appear as part of a larger homebuyer education path, alongside lessons and checklists.

**Rewards and motivation**  
Completing stages can earn users **NestCoins**, which can be tied to real life rewards. This gives users an extra sense of progress and makes learning feel more meaningful and satisfying. 🏆

**Aligned look and feel**  
I have tried to keep the overall look and feel of Paper Trail Sprint similar to the Nest Navigate website. 

This integration helps Paper Trail Sprint feel less like just a game and more like a supportive learning tool within the user’s homebuying journey.

## 📈 Success Metrics

I would measure the success of Paper Trail Sprint by how well it improves clarity, confidence, and usability.

The most important signals of success for me are:

**Completion rate**  
I want to see how many users start the experience and finish at least one stage.

**Return usage**  
I want to know whether users come back to continue later stages or replay the experience to improve their understanding.

**Reduced confusion**  
I want users to say they better understand which documents are needed and when.

**Ease of use feedback**  
This is especially important to me. I want users to describe the experience as clear, simple, and helpful. ❤️

For me, a successful feature is not just one that gets clicks. It is one that helps users feel calmer, more informed, and more in control.

## 🛠️ Technology Rationale

For the prototype, I chose a stack that I am already comfortable building with.

I used **React**, **TypeScript**, **Tailwind CSS**, and **Motion for React**, with **Vercel** for deployment.


I chose this technology stack because it helped me build a prototype that supports three things most:

**Clarity**  
I wanted the interface to feel intuitive and easy to understand.

**Speed**  
I wanted users to be able to start interacting with the feature right away.

**Simplicity**  
I wanted the experience to stay focused on solving the document confusion problem in the most approachable way possible.
